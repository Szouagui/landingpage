Moroccan Lamb shank Tagine

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb shanks
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 can (28 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1/2 cup pitted green olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Season the lamb shanks with salt and pepper.
  3. In a Dutch oven, heat some olive oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Brown the lamb shanks on all sides, about 5-7 minutes per side.
  5. Remove the lamb shanks from the Dutch oven and set aside.
  6. Add the onions to the Dutch oven and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  7. Add the garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  8. Add the spices and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  9. Add the diced tomatoes and chicken broth and bring to a boil.
  10. Return the lamb shanks to the Dutch oven and cover with the sauce.
  11. Add the olives and stir to combine.
  12. Cover the Dutch oven and bake in the preheated oven for 2-2 1/2 hours, until the lamb shanks are tender.
  13. Serve with chopped parsley and cilantro on top.

Moroccan Couscous

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb lamb stew meat
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup couscous
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the lamb and brown on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  2. Remove the lamb from the pot and set aside.
  3. Add the onion to the pot and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the garlic, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, turmeric, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Stir to combine and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
  5. Return the lamb to the pot and add the diced tomatoes, chickpeas, and chicken broth. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and let the stew simmer for 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  7. While the stew is simmering, prepare the couscous according to the package instructions.
  8. Fluff the cooked couscous with a fork and stir in the chopped cilantro.
  9. Season the stew with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. To serve, spoon the couscous onto plates and top with the lamb stew.

Moroccan Harira

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas
  • 1 cup dried lentils
  • 1/2 cup rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh celery
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 6 cups chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste

instructions

  1. Soak chickpeas and lentils overnight in water.
  2. Drain the soaked chickpeas and lentils and add them to a large pot with the rice, onion, garlic, cilantro, parsley, celery, tomatoes, paprika, cumin, turmeric, cinnamon, black pepper, and chicken broth.
  3. Bring the pot to a boil, reduce the heat, and let it simmer for about 1 hour, stirring occasionally.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, flour, and olive oil until smooth.
  5. Add the lemon juice mixture to the pot and stir until well combined.
  6. Continue to simmer the soup for another 15-20 minutes, or until it thickens.
  7. Season with salt to taste and serve hot.
